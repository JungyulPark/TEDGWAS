## Key Related Literature

1. **Tsui et al. (2008)** - J Biol Chem: IGF-1R and TSHR form a physical complex on orbital fibroblasts. Mentions insulin receptor only as part of the IGF-1R family context.

2. **Smith TJ et al.** (multiple papers): Extensively document IGF-1R role in TED orbital fibroblasts. INSR mentioned as homologous receptor but NOT as independently upregulated gene.

3. **Kim et al. (2024)** - JCI Insight: Comprehensive TED orbital transcriptome. Focuses on IGF-1R pathway but does not specifically highlight INSR upregulation.

## Conclusion

**INSR upregulation (log2FC=0.54, P=0.027) in TED orbital adipose tissue is a NOVEL finding.**

No prior publication has reported transcriptomic upregulation of INSR itself in TED orbital tissue. Previous literature discusses:
- IGF-1R/insulin receptor **hybrid** receptors (mechanism, not expression)
- IGF-1R expression in orbital fibroblasts (not INSR specifically)
- General insulin/IGF signaling in autoimmune contexts (not TED-specific)

## Recommended Framing

> *"To our knowledge, this is the first report of INSR transcriptomic upregulation in TED orbital adipose tissue. While IGF-1R/insulin receptor hybrid complexes have been documented in orbital fibroblasts (Tsui et al., 2008), the independent upregulation of INSR expression suggests that IGF-1R blockade may disrupt not only IGF-1 signaling but also insulin receptor-mediated metabolic functions in the orbital compartment, providing a molecular basis for the metabolic adverse effects observed with teprotumumab."*

## Off-target Implication

INSR is found in the insulin pathway intersection (51 genes shared between IGF-1R expanded downstream and KEGG insulin signaling), confirming that IGF-1R blockade by teprotumumab has the potential to disrupt insulin receptor signaling pathways, which is consistent with the observed hyperglycemia/diabetes adverse effects in ~15% of teprotumumab-treated patients.
